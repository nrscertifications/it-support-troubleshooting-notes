# Troubleshooting Note: Android Data Migration and Contact Sync Issue

## Scenario

A user received a new Samsung Android phone and needed help transferring data from an older Android device. The phone had been provided without the user’s personal data transferred, so assistance was needed to migrate the user’s information to the new device.

After the migration, the user reported that contacts from another person were appearing together with their own contacts.

## Symptoms

- User had a new Samsung Android phone that needed setup
- Data from the old phone needed to be transferred to the new phone
- Existing SIM card needed to be moved to the new device
- Contacts from another user/account appeared in the Contacts app
- User needed help removing the incorrect contacts from the phone

## Possible Causes

- The old phone had been used by another person before being passed to the current user
- Another Google or Samsung account was still connected to the device
- Contacts from the previous account were syncing to the phone
- Contact data was mixed because multiple accounts were connected during or after migration
- The default contact/account settings were not reviewed before transferring data

## Troubleshooting Steps

1. Confirmed that the user wanted to transfer data from the old Android phone to the new Samsung Android phone.
2. Connected both phones to the same home Wi-Fi network.
3. Used Samsung Smart Switch to transfer data from the old phone to the new phone.
4. Moved the existing SIM card from the old phone to the new phone.
5. Confirmed the new phone had the user’s transferred data available.
6. Later reviewed the issue when the user reported that another person’s contacts were appearing in the Contacts app.
7. Opened the Contacts app and checked the connected account/contact source.
8. Identified that the old user’s account was still linked to the phone.
9. Removed the previously linked account from the device.
10. Confirmed that the unwanted contacts were no longer displaying after the account was removed.

## Resolution

The user’s data was transferred to the new Samsung Android phone using Samsung Smart Switch over the home Wi-Fi network. The existing SIM card was moved to the new device.

When another person’s contacts appeared on the phone, the issue was traced to a previously linked account that was still syncing contacts to the device. Removing that account from the phone stopped those contacts from appearing in the Contacts app.

## Prevention / Best Practice

Before transferring data from an old phone, check which Google or Samsung accounts are signed in on the device. If the old phone was previously used by another person, remove unrelated accounts before or after migration to prevent contacts, calendars, emails, or other synced data from appearing on the new user’s phone.

When helping a user with account or contact issues, confirm with the user before removing an account from the device. Removing an account from the phone is different from permanently deleting the account itself.
